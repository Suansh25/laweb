Many Languages,One voice: India

India is a land of incredible linguistic diversity, with over 1,600 spoken dialects and more than 21 officially recognized languages. These languages fall into four major families: Indo-Aryan, Dravidian, Austroasiatic, and Tibeto-Burman. The Indo-Aryan family includes languages like Hindi, Bengali, Marathi, and Gujarati, while the Dravidian family comprises Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, and Malayalam. India also boasts six classical languages—Sanskrit, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, and Oriya—each with a rich literary heritage. The country uses a variety of scripts, such as Devanagari for Hindi and Marathi, Bengali script for Bengali and Assamese, and distinct scripts for Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, and Malayalam. This linguistic diversity not only facilitates communication but also reflects India’s rich cultural and historical legacy, with each language contributing to the preservation and promotion of regional traditions and heritage.
